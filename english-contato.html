    • About professor Claudio:

    Professor Claudio holds a Doctor’s degree in Information Science by the University of Brasilia and a Master’s in Computer and Systems Engineering by COPPE-UFRJ (Alberto Luiz Coimbra Graduation and Engineering Research Institute). For 20 years worked at UNESCO - UNITED NATIONS EDUCATION, SCIENTIFIC AND CULTURAL ORGANIZATION (Paris) as an international employee, and worked in more than 20 countries. He has experience in teaching in the field of Foreign Applied Languages, Computer Science, and Information Science, programs for the information society, inclusion of languages in the digital world, automatic treatment of the language, policies for accessibility to the scientific information, digital libraries, policies to make the university more included internationally, projects related to international funding, idealization and organization of international conferences -in the United Nations System in particular. Professor Claudio was Administration of the IBICT (an organ of the Ministry of Science and Technology and Innovation) coordinator until september of 2009. He was also professor at the Federal University of Ceará until october of 2010. He is currently professor from the Department of Foreign Languages and Translation (LET) at UnB. He coordinated the creation and implementation of the Bachelor’s degree in Foreign Languages Applied to the Multilingualism and Information Society (LEA-MSI), of the University of Brasília, and was its first coordinator, from october 2010 to december 2013. He coordinates the UNESCO Chair about “Linguistic Policies for Multilingualism” at UnB.
